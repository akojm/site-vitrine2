Who are we ?

Baby School is a childminder home run by MRS Johnson and MRS Berclay. We are located in the heart of Cenon, a welcoming and safe place for children aged 0 to 3 years old.

A childminder home is a place where one or more approved childminders welcome young children to look after them while their parents work or are away. Unlike a traditional nursery or daycare, a childminder home provides a more intimate, family-friendly environment, where a small group of children are cared for in a domestic setting.

Ours Services

Small groups of children

Childminder homes typically accommodate a small number of children, allowing the childminder to provide more individualized attention to each child. This promotes a closer bond between the childminder and the children, as well as a more family atmosphere.

Personalized educational program

Childminders often develop an educational program adapted to the needs and interests of the children they welcome. This program may include early learning activities, educational games, outings and outdoor activities, aimed at promoting the overall development of each child.

Collaboration with families

Childminders generally maintain close communication with the families of the children they welcome. They share information on the child's development, their needs, their progress, and work with parents to ensure continuity between the home and the childminder's home.

Ours Activity

Artistic activities

  2. Collage with various materials (colored paper, buttons, pieces of fabric, etc.).
  3. Modeling with modeling clay or salt dough.
  4. Dessin libre ou dirigé sur différents supports (papier, carton, feuilles de couleur).

Sensory activities

  1. Sandbox with beach toys.
  2. Exploring different textures with fabrics, crepe paper, sensory balls, etc.
  3. Water games with pools, sprayers and floating toys..

Physical activity

  1. Motor skills course with tunnels, hoops, cones, etc.
  2. Ball games adapted to their age.
  3. Free dance to lively music.
  4. Yoga for children with simple and fun postures.

Cognitive and educational activities

  1. Sorting games with shapes, colors or sizes.
  2. Puzzles adapted to their level of development.
  3. Reading of interactive stories and discussion around the characters and themes covered.
  4. Simple board games promoting sharing and decision-making.

Social and group activities

  1. Cooperative games where children must work together to achieve a common goal.
  2. Simple cooking activities like making smoothies, sandwiches or cookies.
  3. Cooperative games where children must work together to achieve a common goal. Simple cooking activities like making smoothies, sandwiches or cookies. Role-playing games with disguises and accessories allowing children to express their imagination.

Outdoor activities

  1. Games in the sandbox.
  2. Obstacle course in the garden or on a playground.
  3. reasure hunt in the nursery garden.
  4. Observation of nature (insects, leaves, flowers, etc.).
